What This System Does

This system computes classical electromagnetic geometric kernels at every atom of a protein, as full rank-2 tensors. Each tensor decomposes into T0 (isotropic), T1 (antisymmetric), and T2 (symmetric traceless, 5 components).

10 calculators (8 classical + 2 MOPAC-derived) produce full tensor output per atom. An equivariant calibration pipeline tunes ~80 kernel parameters against DFT WT–ALA deltas across 723 proteins. The primary measure is T2 R² — how well the calibrated classical kernels reproduce the angular structure of DFT shielding.

This is not a chemical shift predictor. T0 is what existing predictors compute. This system's contribution is T2: the angular pattern that reveals where classical models succeed and fail.
