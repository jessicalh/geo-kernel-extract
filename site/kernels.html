The Shared Dipolar Kernel

Four of eight calculators share one geometric core:

Kab(d) = 3 da db / r5 − δab / r3

where d = ratom − rsource, r = |d|.

Properties: symmetric, traceless (pure T2, 5 components), units Å−3. This is ∇a∇b(1/r) — the same tensor appearing in magnetic dipole fields, electric field gradients, and any point-source dipolar interaction.

1. Biot-Savart Ring Current

Johnson & Bovey (1958). π-electrons above and below an aromatic ring modelled as two current loops at height ±d from the ring plane. Each carries I/2.

Gab = −nb · Ba · PPM_FACTOR

B is computed by numerical integration of the Biot-Savart law around both loops (SI: metres, amperes → Tesla). G is dimensionless.

T2 structure: G = n ⊗ B is rank-1. T0 = n·B·PPM_FACTOR/3 (the isotropic ring current shift). T1 ≠ 0 (G is not symmetric). T2 depends on the direction of B relative to n, the lobe offset d, and ring geometry. T2 does not depend on intensity I — so T2 constrains the geometric parameters while T0 constrains magnitude.

Fused Rings (TRP)

TRP produces three ring objects: TRP5 (pyrrole, I=−6.72), TRP6 (benzene, I=−12.48), TRP9 (indole perimeter, I=−19.2). BS superposition is exact: T0(TRP5)+T0(TRP6)/T0(TRP9) = 1.000. Verified on 720 proteins (87,234 atom-TRP triplets).

2. Haigh-Mallion Ring Current

Haigh & Mallion (1973). π-electron cloud as a uniformly magnetised disk. Surface integral of the dipolar kernel over the ring polygon, via 7-point Gaussian quadrature on a fan triangulation.

Hab(r) = ∫∫ [3ρaρb/ρ5 − δab/ρ3] dS

H is symmetric and traceless (pure T2). Verified at machine precision (max asymmetry 3.9×10−17, max |Tr| 1.2×10−14). Units: Å−1.

Gab = nb · (H · n)a

The full shielding kernel G is rank-1 (same structure as BS), with non-zero T0, T1, T2.

3. McConnell Bond Anisotropy

McConnell (1957). Each bond has anisotropic magnetic susceptibility, creating a dipolar shielding field. The full tensor (not the commonly cited traceless K):

σab = (Δχ/3) · Mab/r3

where the McConnell tensor Mab is:

Mab = 9 cosθ · d̂a b̂b − 3 b̂a b̂b − (3 d̂a d̂b − δab)

Three terms, three roles:

TermSymmetryIrrep
9 cosθ d̂a b̂basymmetric→ T1
−3 b̂a b̂bsymmetric→ T0
−(3 d̂a d̂b − δab)sym. traceless→ T2 (dipolar kernel K)

T0 = (Δχ/3)(3cos2θ − 1)/r3 — the McConnell isotropic shift. The tensor is asymmetric: T1 ≠ 0.

Per-category decomposition: PeptideCO, PeptideCN, BackboneOther, SidechainCO, Aromatic, SidechainOther — each with separate Δχ. The midpoint_shift parameter moves the effective dipole centre along the bond axis.

4. Coulomb / Buckingham EFG

Buckingham (1960). Partial charges create an E-field and EFG at each atom.

Ea(i) = ke ∑j qj (ri − rj)a / |ri − rj|3
Vab(i) = ke ∑j qj [3(ri−rj)a(ri−rj)b / |ri−rj|5 − δab / |ri−rj|3]

V is the dipolar kernel K weighted by charge and summed — traceless by Gauss's law (pure T2). Decomposed into backbone, sidechain, aromatic, and solvent (APBS minus vacuum) contributions.

σiso = A · Ez + B · Ez2    σT2 = γ · VT2

5. Pi-Quadrupole EFG

Buckingham (1959), Stone T-tensor formalism. The π-electron cloud creates a quadrupole E-field. EFG from a point axial quadrupole Θ along ring normal n:

Gab = 105 dn2 da db / r9 − 30 dn (na db + nb da) / r7 − 15 da db / r7 + 6 na nb / r5 + δab (3/r5 − 15 dn2/r7)

Symmetric, traceless (Laplace), pure T2. Leading radial decay 1/r5. Verified on 723 proteins (1.03M atom-ring pairs, max |Tr| = 5.1×10−15).

6. Ring Susceptibility Anisotropy

McConnell (1957) with b̂ → n̂. Whole-ring diamagnetic susceptibility as a point dipole at ring centre.

σab = (Δχring/3) · [9 cosθ d̂a nb − 3 na nb − (3 d̂a d̂b − δab)] / r3

Same structure as McConnell — asymmetric, non-traceless: T0, T1, T2 all non-zero. Only adjustable parameter is Δχring. No geometric parameter changes the angular shape.

7. London Dispersion

London (1937). Van der Waals 1/r6 summed over ring vertices with CHARMM switching function (Rswitch = 4.3 Å, Rcut = 5.0 Å).

Kdisp,ab = ∑vertices C6 · (3 da db / r8 − δab / r6)

T2 present but typically dominated by McConnell and ring current T2.

8. H-Bond Dipolar

Barfield & Karplus (1969), Cornilescu & Bax (1999). Dipolar tensor to H-bond partner (DSSP Kabsch-Sander criterion). Same derivation as McConnell with b̂ → ĥ (H-bond direction).

σab = (η/3) · [9 cosθ d̂a hb − 3 ha hb − (3 d̂a d̂b − δab)] / r3

Asymmetric, non-traceless. T0, T1, T2 all non-zero.

9 & 10. MOPAC-Derived Calculators

9. MOPAC Coulomb EFG

Same Coulomb kernel as Calculator 4, but charges are PM7 Mulliken charges from MOPAC instead of ff14SB fixed charges. Captures charge polarisation that the fixed-charge force field cannot.

T2 independence verified: MopacCoulomb vs ff14SB Coulomb |cos| = 0.85. Highly correlated but not redundant — 15% angular difference from charge polarisation.

10. MOPAC McConnell (Bond-Order-Weighted)

Same McConnell kernel as Calculator 3, but each bond's contribution weighted by MOPAC Wiberg bond order. Double bonds dominate over single bonds; the relative contributions change, rotating the total T2 pattern.

totalab = ∑bonds bond_orderk · Mab,k / rk3

T2 independence: MopacMcConnell vs unweighted McConnell |cos| = 0.55. Substantially different angular patterns — bond-order weighting produces genuinely different structure.
