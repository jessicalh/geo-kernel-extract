Yates, J. R. and Bartók, A. P. (2024) Accurate predictions of chemical shifts with the rSCAN and r2SCAN mGGA exchange–correlation functionals. Faraday Discuss., 2025, 255, 192–202. DOI: 10.1039/d4fd00142g. 11 pages. 28 references. Funding: Collaborative Computational Project for NMR Crystallography (CCP-NC), UKCP Consortium, CASTEP-USER grant, EPSRC (EP/M022501/1, EP/P022561/1, EP/W030438/1), and HPCMidlands+ (EP/T022108/1).

This paper is a benchmarking study evaluating the regularised meta-generalised gradient approximation (mGGA) functionals rSCAN and r2SCAN for predicting solid-state NMR magnetic shieldings. The authors compare DFT-calculated isotropic shieldings against experimental chemical shifts for a curated set of inorganic oxides and halides. The central argument is that replacing the widely used PBE GGA functional with these mGGAs yields slopes closer to the theoretically expected value of −1 and reduces root mean square deviations, thereby improving the reliability of spectrum assignments for complex inorganic materials. The study is positioned as a methodological calibration note for practitioners using the GIPAW approach in solid-state NMR crystallography.

Calculations employ the gauge-including projector-augmented wave (GIPAW) method within the CASTEP plane-wave pseudopotential code. Structures sourced from the ICSD undergo full geometry optimisation and cell parameter relaxation. Ultrasoft pseudopotentials are generated using the RSCAN functional due to numerical instabilities observed during r2SCAN pseudopotential construction. A consistent 800 eV plane-wave cutoff, a fine grid four times denser than the basis set, and a 0.03 Å⁻¹ k-point spacing are applied. The conversion from shielding to chemical shift uses a linear reference fit. Computational cost is assessed by comparing wall-clock times for small (6-atom MgF₂) and large (256-atom GeSe) systems.

PBE consistently overestimates the slope of the shielding-shift correlation across nuclei: −1.146 for ¹⁹F, −1.135 for ¹⁷O, −1.092 for ³⁵/³⁷Cl, and −1.147 for ⁷⁹/⁸¹Br. rSCAN and r2SCAN bring these slopes closer to −1, with bromine remaining the most affected at −1.074 and −1.063 respectively. When fitted to a fixed gradient of −1, rSCAN and r2SCAN reduce RMSEs to approximately half those of PBE across fluorides, oxides, chlorides, and bromides. The largest residual errors occur for calcium compounds (CaF₂, CaO, CaCl₂, CaBr₂), where the SCAN family fails to adequately describe unoccupied Ca d-orbitals. Applying a 1.25 eV upward shift to the Ca d-levels in the pseudopotential significantly improves agreement, particularly for ¹⁷O. Computationally, rSCAN requires roughly twice the wall-clock time of PBE for small cells but only 1.25 times for large cells, as pseudopotential and FFT operations dominate the cost at scale.

The work extends prior observations of PBE slope deviations for halides and oxides reported by Zheng et al., Griffin et al., and Laskowski and Blaha. It builds on Sadoc’s identification of pseudopotential-dependent errors in fluorides and Profeta et al.’s earlier work on d-orbital corrections. The study positions rSCAN and r2SCAN as numerically stable alternatives to the original SCAN functional, referencing the constraint-satisfying design of Sun et al. and the regularisation strategy of Bartók and Yates. It also engages with the broader GIPAW methodology literature (Pickard and Mauri; Yates et al.) and functional benchmarking studies (Gautam and Carter; Chen et al.).

For the thesis, this paper provides a methodological reference for selecting exchange–correlation functionals when generating DFT calibration data for NMR parameters. The results indicate that mGGA functionals capture the shielding-shift correlation more accurately than PBE, reducing systematic slope deviations and lowering RMSEs. The persistent error in calcium-containing systems highlights a functional-specific limitation that may require pseudopotential adjustment rather than functional replacement. While the paper reports isotropic values and linear fits rather than full tensor components, it confirms that higher-rung functionals improve the signal captured in DFT shieldings. The modest computational overhead for larger systems supports their use in generating training data for kernel calibration, provided the dataset composition accounts for known functional biases. The study does not address rank-2 tensor preservation or kernel construction, but its findings on functional-dependent correlation quality are directly relevant to calibrating the mapping from geometric kernels to shielding values.
