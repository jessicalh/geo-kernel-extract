Bånkestad, M., Dorst, K. M., Widmalm, G., & Rönnols, J. (2023). Carbohydrate NMR chemical shift predictions using E(3) equivariant graph neural networks. arXiv:2311.12657v1 [cs.LG], 14 pages, approximately 30 references. DOI not provided in text; arXiv identifier 2311.12657v1. Supported by the Swedish Research Council (grant 2022-03014) and the Knut and Alice Wallenberg Foundation; computations performed on the MeluXina supercomputer.

This is a research paper introducing GeqShift, an E(3) equivariant graph neural network designed to predict isotropic 1H and 13C NMR chemical shifts for carbohydrates. The authors argue that accurate prediction requires explicit three-dimensional molecular geometry and equivariance to Euclidean transformations, particularly for molecules with complex stereochemistry. The work targets computational chemistry and machine learning audiences, positioning the model as a data-driven alternative to database-matching programs like CASPER and computationally expensive DFT calculations.

The model processes molecular graphs where nodes represent carbon and oxygen atoms (hydrogens are excluded) and edges connect atoms within a 6 Å cutoff. Input features include atom type, connected hydrogen count, bond type, and relative distance embeddings. The network employs seven E(3)-equivariant self-attention layers using the e3nn library, with hidden representations comprising scalar (ℓ=0), vector (ℓ=1, odd parity), and rank-2 tensor (ℓ=2, even parity) components. To account for conformational dynamics, each molecule is represented by an ensemble of 100 conformations generated via RDKit’s ETKDGv3 method and filtered using the MMFF94 force field. Training minimizes the mean absolute error across the ensemble, with predictions averaged post-inference.

On a ten-fold cross-validated dataset of 375 carbohydrates (107 monosaccharides, 153 disaccharides, 115 trisaccharides) containing 5356 1H and 4713 13C shifts, the full ensemble model achieves a mean absolute error of 0.31 ppm for 13C and 0.032 ppm for 1H. Root mean square errors for monosaccharides are 0.58 ppm and 0.057 ppm, respectively. Prediction errors are approximately zero-centered with standard deviations of 0.39 ppm (13C) and 0.052 ppm (1H). The model outperforms the baseline SG-IMP-IR network by roughly threefold for 13C and twofold for 1H, and surpasses the DFT-based benchmark reported by Palivec et al. (2022), which recorded MAEs of 1.11 ppm and 0.081 ppm. Extension to two tetrasaccharide polysaccharides yields MAEs of 0.56 ppm (13C) and 0.052 ppm (1H).

The paper builds on the CASPER program for experimental shift data (Lundborg & Widmalm, 2011; Jansson et al., 2006) and the scalable GNN architecture SG-IMP-IR (Han et al., 2022). It situates its methodology within the broader development of Euclidean neural networks (Geiger & Smidt, 2022) and equivariant attention transformers (Liao & Smidt, 2023). The authors compare performance against prior machine learning studies summarized by Jonas et al. (2022) and Unzueta et al. (2021), as well as DFT simulations of saccharide shifts in aqueous environments (Palivec et al., 2022).

The study demonstrates that equivariant geometric representations combined with conformational ensembles capture stereochemical dependencies in carbohydrate NMR shifts. While the output is restricted to isotropic scalars rather than full shielding tensors, the internal use of ℓ=2 even parity tensors indicates that rank-2 geometric information is utilized during message passing. The work supports the broader premise that three-dimensional atomic arrangement and symmetry constraints are necessary for accurate shift prediction. However, it does not address shielding tensor calibration, kernel decomposition, or protein structural contexts, so its direct methodological transfer to the current project is limited to the validation of 3D equivariance and ensemble averaging strategies.
