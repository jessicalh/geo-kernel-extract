Sahakyan, A.B. & Vendruscolo, M. (2013) "Analysis of the Contributions of Ring Current and Electric Field Effects to the Chemical Shifts of RNA Bases." The Journal of Physical Chemistry B 117, 1989–1998. DOI: 10.1021/jp3057306. Received 12 June 2012, published 11 February 2013. 10 pages, 47 references. No declared competing financial interest. Supported by the Herchel Smith Fund, Darwin College, Schlumberger Research (ABS), BBSRC (MV). Cambridge Department of Chemistry. Acknowledges helpful discussion with Prof. Roger Mallion.

The paper extends the structural-NMR apparatus that Case 1995 established for proteins to RNA bases. Three ring-current models — Pople 1956 point-dipole, Waugh-Fessenden-Johnson-Bovey 1957/1958 loop-current, and Haigh-Mallion 1972/1980 Hückel surface-integral — are compared under a unified sign convention (previously inconsistent across papers). Ring-current and electric-field contributions to ¹H, ¹³C, ¹⁵N, and ¹⁷O chemical shifts are calibrated using hybrid DFT on a new database of RNA dibase geometries.

Method. The authors build DiBaseRNA, a structural database of inter-ring arrangements — adjacent (ADJ), spatial (SPT), and hydrogen-bonded (HBD) — extracted from the Richardson RNA05 X-ray database (9486 nt content, ≤3.0 Å). Hybrid DFT shielding uses PBE1PBE/6-311+G(2d,p)/GIAO in Gaussian03. Each dibase gets three shielding calculations: the full pair, plus each base in isolation with its neighbor replaced by a dummy placeholder (to retrieve the electric field values at the correct positions). Linear models are fit in R under three schemes: ring current only, electric field only, and joint RC+EF, separately for 5- and 6-membered rings and each atom type.

Results. Haigh-Mallion slightly improves over Pople, but the gap is small enough that the authors derive empirical interconversion equations between Pople and HM geometric factors — letting fast Pople implementations recover HM-level accuracy. For ¹H, RC+EF reaches R=0.961 (rms 0.079 ppm). For heavier nuclei, electric-field effects dominate: ¹³C gives R=0.702 from EF alone versus 0.257 from RC alone; ¹⁷O is extreme, with electric-field sensitivity up to 80 ppm. Excluding hydrogen-bonded dibases, the overall effect of a neighboring ring on ¹H/¹⁵N/¹³C can reach 4.84/34.49/27.18 ppm (averages 0.35/1.75/2.60). The authors flag a methodological point: ring normals computed from three ring atoms are unstable to out-of-plane atomic fluctuations in MD, and they propose averaging two independently computed normals.

Implications for our work. This is the RNA parallel of what we are building for proteins, and it validates the per-element hierarchy our Stage 1 ridge regression observed: electric-field kernels dominate for heavy nuclei (C, N, O), ring currents for H. Their ring-normal stability concern applies directly to our `BiotSavartResult` and `HaighMallionResult` implementations — our codebase uses fixed 3-atom ring-normal definitions, which should be audited for the same instability in Stage 2 MD trajectories where frame-to-frame ring wobble may bias T2 predictions. Three axes of thesis extension beyond this paper: (i) rank-2 tensor outputs with T0/T1/T2 decomposition (they use isotropic shifts only); (ii) r²SCAN meta-GGA rather than PBE1PBE hybrid as the DFT reference — a functional-choice discussion worth surfacing in the methods chapter; (iii) explicit-solvent MD averaging rather than standardised geometries. The DiBaseRNA geometry-generation strategy (standardised base geometries rotated to match X-ray arrangements) is a useful methodological pointer for disentangling geometry-noise from kernel-physics contributions.
