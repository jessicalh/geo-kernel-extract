**4.1 NUS reconstructions (cont).**
- EDHRN [116] — two parallel subnetworks at different resolutions; bidirectional activation flow between them. Particularly effective for reconstructing weak NMR signals. Trained on synthetic with exp + Poisson gap.
- FID-Net [3] — WaveNet-inspired: dilated convolutions + Gated Activation Units (GAUs) + residual connections. Input: sparse NMR data Fourier-transformed in direct (or ¹H-¹⁵N plane) dimension. Trained on simulated only, validated on experimental. Comparable to SMILE and hmsIST methods on high-quality data; surpasses them as input quality deteriorates.

**4.2 Virtual decoupling.**
Context: dense ¹H networks in solid-state → strong ¹H-¹H dipolar → need homo- and hetero-nuclear decoupling. Solution-state scalar interactions carry structural info but hinder peak identification. ¹³C-¹³C one-bond couplings (35-55 Hz) in ¹³C-detected experiments complicate spectra. Homonuclear decoupling is tricky experimentally, so "virtual decoupling" (ML-based) is an alternative.
- FID-Net follow-up [4]: three new FID-Net instances for ¹³C-¹³C side-chain, CON (¹³CO-¹⁵N), ¹³Cα-¹³CO correlation. Trained on synthetic, validated on experimental. Advantage over IPAP [124] and S3E [125]: single spectrum instead of multiple.
- PIPNet [119] — "infinite MAS" target: inferring isotropic 1D spectra that would theoretically be obtained at infinite spinning rates. Solid-state context: even highest MAS rates can't fully eliminate inter-proton dipolar couplings. Convolutional LSTM sequence-processing — spectrum-at-lowest-MAS processed first, refined through increasing-MAS sequence. Ensemble of 16 LSTMs (independent synthetic training sets). Trained on millions of synthetic datasets. Applied to 8 molecular solids: linewidths as narrow as 57 Hz — surpasses current fast-MAS hardware limits. PIPNet2D [126] extends to 2D.

**4.3 Denoising.**
Context: noise from thermal fluctuations, hardware imperfections, limited acquisition time. Traditional techniques trade off weak-signal preservation vs. noise suppression. DL approaches "preserve fine spectral details."
- AC-ResNet [5] — 72-layer ResNet with addition + concatenation operations (reduces overfitting). Custom loss: SM-CDMANE (Signal Matrix Matched Continuously Differentiable Mean Absolute Normalized Error) — amplifies sensitivity to weak signals. Simulated training only. Enhances Zangger-Sterk pure-shift spectra; restores high quality from noisy low-scan experiments. Benchmarked against 36/72/108-layer ResNet variants.
- DN-UNet [2] — U-Net adaptation (medical image segmentation origin). 12 layers 1D, 9 layers 2D/3D. M-to-S training: multiple noisy examples per ground-truth spectrum. Simulated training. Applied to 1D and multidim by 1D slicing. Key finding: denoising works with as few as **4 scans** vs. 1024 scan reference, or **0.02 mol/L** vs. 0.5 mol/L reference. Predecessor NASR [129] inferior. Can enhance SNR more than **200-fold** in favourable conditions.
- Medical-domain ML spillover: SENNet [120] for separating small vs. macromolecule signals in plasma/serum ¹H NMR. U-Net 1D variant. Principal-component analysis confirms extracted spectra match relaxation-edited and diffusion-edited spectral editing methods. Also: ¹H-MRS brain metabolite quantification (NAA, glutamate, myo-inositol, GABA) uses CNNs [130].

**5. Conclusions and outlook.**

**Overall framing:** ML expected to transform NMR, yet full potential not realized. Beyond architectures already adapted, **vision transformers** [131] and **diffusion models** [132] hold promise. Open challenges: data scarcity, model generalizability, interpretability.

**5.1 ML adoption in lab practice.** Heterogeneity of NMR data (systems, spectrometers, labs, experimental setups) complicates trained-model application. Fragmented standalone ML solutions create maintenance burden. Platform-based solutions (NMRtist, NMRBox [132]) emerging.

**5.2 Reducing experimental input.** Goal: chemical shift assignment + structure determination + dynamics in 24 h, using 2D ¹H-¹H NOESY + ¹H-¹H TOCSY/COSY (possibly at 1.2 GHz ultra-high field or super-resolution [133]). Enables sophisticated 2D NMR on eukaryotic-expression proteins where sample is limited.

**5.3 NMR experiment design optimization for deep learning analysis.** Paradigm shift: design pulse sequences optimized for DL analysis, not human analysis. Super-resolution NMR [133] integrated into HNCA is a recent example. Dynamic spectrometer-analysis interplay (targeted acquisition [134]) — acquisition continues until required spectral features are resolved or software declares no more data needed.

**5.4 Spectra databases.** 14,500 PDB NMR structures; similar BMRB chemical shift depositions. BUT time-domain/spectral data of proteins deposited in <1.5% of BMRB entries — blocks deep learning progress. ARTINA trained with only 100 proteins [6,67] is remarkable for that reason. NMRPrime database [135] established for this; BMRB also accepts time-domain data [136].

**5.5 Toward comprehensive structure and dynamic determination.** Framing: NMR signature yields thousands of probes (shifts, NOEs, couplings, relaxation) within a few 2D/3D experiments. Goal is to find a structural+dynamical representation of the protein consistent with all this probe data — ML as the integration engine.
