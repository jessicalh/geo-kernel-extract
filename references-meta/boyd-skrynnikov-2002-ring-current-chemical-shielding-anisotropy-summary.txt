Boyd, J. & Skrynnikov, N.R. (2002) "Calculations of the Contribution of Ring Currents to the Chemical Shielding Anisotropy." Journal of the American Chemical Society 124(9), 1832–1833. DOI: 10.1021/ja016476f. Published web 07 February 2002, received 22 June 2001. 2 pages (JACS Communication). Department of Biochemistry and Oxford Centre for Molecular Sciences, University of Oxford (Boyd); Department of Medical Genetics, University of Toronto (Skrynnikov). Supported by OCMS (BBSRC, EPSRC, MRC); Skrynnikov by CIHR Centennial Fellowship. Cited as A8 in our annotated bibliography.

A two-page JACS Communication that extends the Johnson-Bovey ring-current formula from the isotropic shift to the full chemical shielding anisotropy (CSA) tensor. The authors derive a new equation (eq 2 of the paper) for the σ_xz component of the ring-current contribution to the shielding tensor — explicitly "reported for the first time" in their framing — and combine it with Johnson-Bovey's σ_zz (eq 1) to give the full rank-2 shielding-tensor correction from a ring-current source.

Method. The total shielding decomposes as σ = σ_local + σ_rc, where σ_rc is the ring-current contribution and σ_local includes everything else. Two atomic models with an NMA (N-methyl acetamide) probe molecule near either a benzene ring (full-ring system) or a 1,3-cyclohexadiene (CYH) ring (an "olefinic analogue" that models local effects without π-ring-current). Full DFT shielding calculations via Gaussian98 at B3LYP/6-311++G(2d,p) with GIAO; ring radius a = 1.39 Å, intensity factor I = 1. σ_rc from eqs 1-2 in the ring frame (cylindrical (ρ, z) coordinates, complete elliptic integrals K and E); the formula is the same double-loop Johnson-Bovey construction at ±0.64 Å above and below the ring plane. Proof-of-concept on 1HN of NMA at 3.65 Å above benzene and 4.0 Å at 54.7° off-axis, with NMA rotation Ω swept from 0° to 90° to vary the relative contributions of σ_local and σ_rc.

Results. Closed-form σ_local + σ_rc (from eqs 1-2 applied to NMA-CYH + σ_rc) closely tracks the full DFT-on-NMA-benzene answer across the rotation sweep, validating the additive decomposition at rank-2-tensor level. Isotropic shielding differs by 0.99 ± 0.05 ppm (benzene) and -0.05 ± 0.14 ppm (CYH), matching the σ_rc values 1.04 and 0 ppm respectively. Application to the fibronectin type-2 module (ref 11): residue G42 ¹HN shows maximum CSA of 16.6 ppm, dominated by σ_rc from a N-H⋯π hydrogen bond with F19. DFT on the G42 fragment gives ∆σ = 18.2 ppm, reasonable agreement with (σ_local + σ_rc). For backbone ¹³C' and ¹⁵N the CSA contribution from ring currents is typically <1 ppm rms.

Implications for our work. Direct two-page proof-of-concept that Johnson-Bovey style ring-current formulas produce the full rank-2 shielding tensor, not just the isotropic trace. Paper A8 in our annotated bibliography for this reason. Three takeaways: (i) the σ_xz formula (eq 2 of the paper) is a specific closed-form result we could compare our `BiotSavartResult` tensor output against at benchmark geometries — direct idea-8 sanity-check material. (ii) The N-H⋯π hydrogen bond at G42 gives a 16.6 ppm CSA contribution from σ_rc alone; this is an order-of-magnitude anchor for aromatic-H-bond environments in our per-atom Stage 2 predictions. (iii) The σ_local + σ_rc decomposition (σ_local via NMA-CYH as olefinic analogue, σ_rc via the analytical formula) is the exact paradigm our thesis generalises: classical kernel contribution + DFT remainder, for every kernel family rather than just ring current. Useful cite for the methods chapter.
