This benchmarking research paper evaluates fragment-based electronic structure methods for predicting 13C and 15N chemical shielding anisotropy (CSA) tensors in molecular crystals. The authors argue that pairwise fragment calculations, augmented by electrostatic embedding and hybrid density functionals, can reproduce principal tensor components with accuracy comparable to or exceeding periodic plane-wave GIPAW implementations. The work targets computational chemists and solid-state NMR practitioners, positioning fragment approaches as a computationally efficient pathway for calibrating shielding tensors in systems where periodic boundary conditions are impractical or prohibitively expensive.

The methodology decomposes the shielding tensor via a two-body many-body expansion, truncating after pairwise interactions while modeling long-range crystal polarization through the self-consistent reproduction of the Madelung potential (SCRMP) using iteratively updated CHELPG point charges. Calculations employ gauge-including atomic orbitals (GIAO) with a locally dense basis set (6-311+G(2d,p) on the asymmetric unit and a 2 Å shell, 6-311G(d,p) at 2–4 Å, and 6-31G beyond 4 Å). Seven density functionals are tested (PBE, BLYP, OPBE, TPSS, TPSSh, PBE0, B3LYP) against experimental principal components. Linear regression (δ_ii = Aσ_ii + B) maps absolute DFT shieldings to experimental shifts, capturing the signal relationship between calculated and observed components. A cluster/fragment variant adds explicit many-body contributions within a 4 Å radius.

The benchmark covers 432 13C principal components across 23 crystals (~250 ppm range) and 45 15N components across 6 crystals (>400 ppm range). For 13C, hybrid functionals (PBE0, B3LYP) yield RMSEs of ~4.05–4.13 ppm, outperforming GGA functionals (~4.76–4.90 ppm) and GIPAW PBE (4.98 ppm) by ~0.6–0.8 ppm. Two-body cutoffs converge at 4–6 Å, and adding explicit many-body terms via the cluster/fragment model provides negligible improvement for carbon. Regression slopes for principal components deviate from the ideal -1 by 0.2–4.4%, compensating for basis set and embedding errors. Cross-testing regression parameters between isotropic shifts and principal components shows strong consistency for 13C (RMSE increase <0.3 ppm) but larger deviations for 15N (~0.6–1.3 ppm), indicating the smaller nitrogen dataset and greater sensitivity to local polarization.

The paper builds directly on Hartman et al. [11, 15, 23], extending prior isotropic shift benchmarks to full tensor components and upgrading electrostatic embedding from GDMA to SCRMP. It engages with Holmes et al. [13], confirming hybrid functional superiority for 13C but contradicting earlier reports of meta-GGA (TPSS/TPSSh) dominance for 15N CSA tensors. The methodology aligns with periodic DFT benchmarks by Pickard and Mauri [9] and Yates et al. [10], while referencing experimental tensor compilations from Kalakewich et al. [3] and Soss et al. [27]. For the present project, this work validates the use of hybrid functionals (PBE0, B3LYP) and linear regression for mapping DFT shielding tensors to experimental values, particularly demonstrating that regression parameters derived from isotropic shifts can reliably scale principal components for 13C. The convergence of fragment calculations at 4–6 Å and the modest impact of explicit many-body terms on 13C tensors suggest that pairwise fragment models are sufficient for capturing the dominant signal in carbon shielding. The 15N results highlight greater sensitivity to local polarization and many-body effects, which may inform per-element complexity assignments in calibration stages.
