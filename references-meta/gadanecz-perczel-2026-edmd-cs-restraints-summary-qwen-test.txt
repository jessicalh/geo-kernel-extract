Gadanecz, M.; Fazekas, Z.; Menyhárd, D. K.; Perczel, A. Improving Protein Structure Determination by Integrating Ensemble-Driven Molecular Dynamics with Chemical Shift-Based Restraints. J. Chem. Inf. Model. 2026, 66, 2844−2856. DOI: 10.1021/acs.jcim.5c02358. 13 pages. 52 references. Funding: Hungarian NRDI Fund (Project 2018−1.2.1-NKP-2018−00005 and 2020−1.1.6-JÖVO-2021−00010), EU RRF (RRF-2.3.1−21−2022−00015), and Horizon 2020 iNEXT-Discovery (project 871037).

This research paper presents a computational protocol for refining protein structures using backbone chemical shifts as the sole experimental input. The authors benchmark Ensemble-driven Molecular Dynamics (EDMD) on five globular proteins (13.1–19.2 kDa) spanning CATH classes, four of which contain nonprotein cofactors or ligands. The work argues that deriving continuous, differentiable torsional potentials from structural ensembles improves conformational sampling, ligand placement, and restraint fulfillment compared to standard molecular dynamics or unrestrained refinement pipelines.

The method builds on CS-Rosetta Abrelax calculations, which generate 10,000 models from sequence and chemical shifts without NOE restraints. Kernel density estimation and Boltzmann inversion are applied to the φ and ψ dihedral angle distributions of the top-scoring models, weighted by Rosetta scores, to construct potential energy functions (PEFs) at T = 310 K using the relation PEF(i) = RT × ln(Pi(α)). The negative first derivative of these PEFs replaces the AMBER-ff99SBildnp*45 torsional terms. Two parallel 1000 ns GROMACS 2022.2 simulations are run for each system, with the second 500 ns analyzed as the refined ensemble. Missing cofactors (RTL, FMN, GDP+Mg²⁺, NADP) are reinserted from reference structures or docking before solvation with OPC water and ion neutralization.

EDMD consistently reduces backbone RMSD relative to reference crystal or NMR structures. For converged CS-Rosetta calculations (CRBP, FBP, KRAS), the average improvement is 0.3 Å; for nonconverged systems (RTT103, THdIII), it is 4.6 Å. NOE distance restraint fulfillment increases by 12% for converged models and 39% for nonconverged models. Standard MD simulations without the modified force field occasionally sample incorrect conformations, such as EF-loop penetration in CRBP or FMN ribityl chain displacement in FBP, which EDMD avoids. LoCoHD analysis confirms that local chemical environments are preserved. Parallel simulations show high reproducibility, and temperature testing at 350 K yields similar convergence behavior.

The protocol extends the authors’ previous csdMD method (Gadanecz et al., 2023) and integrates concepts from CS-Rosetta (Shen et al., 2008, 2009; Lange & Baker, 2012), Monte Carlo chemical shift folding (Robustelli et al., 2009), and iterative Rosetta-MD refinement (Leelananda & Lindert, 2020). It contrasts with NOE-dependent pipelines like CYANA (Güntert, 2015) and ARTINA (Klukowski et al., 2022). Validation and analysis tools cited include MDAnalysis (Michaud-Agrawal et al., 2011; Gowers et al., 2016), LoCoHD (Fazekas et al., 2024), and standard force fields (Aliev et al., 2014; Wang et al., 2004, 2006; Steinbrecher et al., 2012).

The paper does not address NMR shielding tensors, geometric kernel construction, or DFT-based calibration. Its focus is on backbone torsion sampling and ligand reinsertion using chemical shifts as structural restraints. The refined protein ensembles and the explicit handling of cofactor binding pockets could serve as input coordinates for calculating geometric kernels, but the work provides no data on tensor preservation, shielding prediction, or calibration against quantum mechanical values. It is useful as a background reference for structure preparation pipelines, not as a methodological ancestor for the kernel-shielding framework.
