(2010) "Time Averaging of NMR Chemical Shifts in the MLF Peptide in the Solid State." Journal of the American Chemical Society 132, 5993–6000. DOI: 10.1021/ja9062629. Published 13 April 2010; received 26 July 2009. 8 pages, approximately 74 references. Multi-institution authorship: Cambridge TCM group, Max-Planck Biophysical Chemistry (Göttingen), ICREA Barcelona, Oxford, UCL, MIT. Computational resources from Cambridge HPC and Oxford Supercomputing. Bob Griffin (MIT MAS NMR) acknowledged.

The paper quantifies how much conformational and vibrational averaging closes the gap between experimental and DFT-computed ¹³C chemical shifts, using N-formyl-L-methionyl-L-leucyl-L-phenylalanine-OMe (MLF) tripeptide in its orthorhombic P2₁2₁2₁ crystal as a model system. Four calculation modes are compared against MAS solid-state NMR shifts: PR (X-ray heavy-atom positions, H-relaxed), TR (all atoms relaxed), MD (averaged over a 2 ps first-principles Car-Parrinello-style MD trajectory), and cMD (averaged via 10 representative rotameric conformers identified from a 5 μs classical MD simulation).

Method. GIPAW (gauge-including projector augmented wave) DFT shielding in CASTEP — PBE functional, plane waves to 29 Ry cutoff, ultrasoft pseudopotentials, 1 k-point, energy tolerance 1×10⁻⁵ eV. The four molecules in the unit cell are treated as independent chemical environments. First-principles MD: 2 ps, 2 fs timestep, NVT at 300 K with Nosé-Hoover thermostat. Classical MD: GROMACS4, OPLS-AA all-atom force field, NPT at 300 K/1 atm, PME electrostatics, P-LINCS bond constraints. Rotameric conformers selected by clustering on χ1-Met, χ3-Met, χ1-Leu, and χ2-Leu side-chain dihedrals across the cumulative 5 μs trajectory.

Results. RMSD to experimental ¹³C shifts: PR 3.8 ppm, TR 4.2 ppm (worse than PR), MD 3.3 ppm, cMD 3.0 ppm. Pearson correlations all ≥ 0.999. Ten of 22 ¹³C atoms are within 1 ppm after cMD, versus four after MD alone. Mechanistic findings: C=O, Cα–N, and other bond lengths oscillate with ~20 fs periods and amplitudes up to 0.2 Å in first-principles MD, producing instantaneous ¹³C shift fluctuations up to 20 ppm that average out within ~200 fs. Rotameric side-chain dynamics contribute on μs timescales — hence the 5 μs cMD sampling. Residual large errors (Leu Cδ1/Cδ2 up to 7 ppm, Phe Cε1/Cζ ~6 ppm) are attributed to incomplete rotameric sampling or OPLS-AA inaccuracies.

Implications for our work. This is the direct Stage 2 motivator. MD time-averaging improves DFT ¹³C accuracy from 4 ppm (static) to 3 ppm (5 μs averaged) — a 25% improvement, with further progress requiring ms-scale sampling the authors could not afford. Our kit differs substantially: solution-phase proteins with cluster DFT at r²SCAN in ORCA rather than periodic GIPAW+PBE; 25 ns AMBER ff14SB MD rather than 5 μs OPLS-AA; hundreds of atoms per protein rather than 22 carbons in MLF; full rank-2 shielding tensors with T0/T1/T2 decomposition rather than isotropic shifts. Two points transfer directly: (i) fast bond-length vibrations average out within ~200 fs, which justifies ps-spaced MD snapshots without explicit zero-point corrections in our trajectory averaging; (ii) residual errors after averaging trace to rotameric exchange and force-field inaccuracies, not DFT — the same pattern our Stage 1 per-atom-type analysis shows. Cite as: static DFT caps at ~4 ppm error; MD averaging recovers ~1 ppm; the remaining gap is a dynamics-completeness problem.
