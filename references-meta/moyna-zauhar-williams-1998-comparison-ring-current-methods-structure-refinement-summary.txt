Moyna, G., Zauhar, R.J., Williams, H.J., Nachman, R.J. & Scott, A.I. (1998) "Comparison of Ring Current Methods for Use in Molecular Modeling Refinement of NMR Derived Three-Dimensional Structures." Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Sciences 38, 702–709. Received 2 March 1998. 8 pages, 44 references. Texas A&M Center for Biological NMR (A.I. Scott group), Tripos Associates, USDA Veterinary Entomology. NIH and NSF CHE-9528196 support. Acknowledges David A. Case for experimental chemical-shift data and method-comparison guidance.

A systematic comparison of three ring-current methods — Haigh-Mallion, Johnson-Bovey, and the classical point-dipole approximation — for protein ¹H chemical-shift prediction. The paper reparametrizes the point-dipole proportionality constant against a much larger dataset than the original Perkins & Dwek 1980 fit, implements the model as a pseudoenergy penalty term in SYBYL 6.3 for NMR-constrained structure refinement, and demonstrates the value on a small tripeptide.

Method. Two protein sets: 10 proteins (2992 proton assignments, Hα and side chain) for method comparison; expanded to 20 proteins (11,406 data points) for reparameterization. Shifts from Ösapay & Case 1991. Case's SHIFTS 2.2 program is modified to support point-dipole evaluation. Ring-current intensity factors are held constant at Case 1995 values across models — isolating the geometric-factor differences between methods. Linear regression relates the point-dipole spatial function G_PD(r) to HM and JB estimates, giving new constants B = 21.82 ppm (against HM) and 25.44 ppm (against JB), versus Perkins & Dwek's 27.41. The reparametrized point-dipole model is implemented as E_σ = K_σ(σ_calc − σ_obs)² in the Tripos Force Field Engine.

Results. Correlations and RMS on the 2992-proton set: HM r = 0.854, RMS 0.220 ppm; JB r = 0.846, RMS 0.228 ppm; PD with new B (HM-fitted) r = 0.846, RMS 0.226 ppm; Perkins-Dwek PD r = 0.841, RMS 0.235 ppm. Model quality ranks HM > JB ≥ PD-new > PD-old, with ~5% RMS gap between methods. Ranking is preserved on Hα and side-chain subsets. Demonstration: a D-amino-acid tripeptide Fmoc-dPro-dPro-dThr-NH₂ (insect kinin analogue) constrained by NOEs alone gives 10 conformers with RMSD 1.2 Å and mixed cis/trans peptide bonds; adding the chemical-shift pseudoenergy tightens the ensemble to RMSD 0.2 Å, all-trans, with a β-turn matching the natural kinin active core.

Implications for our work. Two useful points for Stage 1 framing. First, the ranking HM > JB > point-dipole is stable across datasets, with modest (~5% RMS) quantitative differences — our ridge regression's kernel weighting should recover a similar ordering if it is picking up physics rather than noise. Second, all three models fit protein ¹H shifts to roughly 0.22 ppm RMS against experimental data; this is the scalar-shift accuracy floor for classical ring-current kernels at the 1998 state of the art, useful as a comparison benchmark when we report DFT-calibrated tensor-kernel residuals on our dataset. The pseudoenergy-penalty framework for shift-constrained structure refinement (the SHIFTY / SHIFTX lineage of Wishart, Case, and Ösapay) is methodologically adjacent but not part of our thesis direction.
