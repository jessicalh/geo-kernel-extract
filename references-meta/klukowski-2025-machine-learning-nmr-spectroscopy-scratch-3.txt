**3.1 Chemical shifts in proteins — covers the reconnaissance predictors plus state-of-the-art.**
- Foundation: PROSHIFT, SPARTA+ (both FF-NN 1 hidden layer + handcrafted features), ShiftX2 (regression trees + alignment). All solution NMR ¹H/¹³C/¹⁵N on backbone + partial side-chain.
- **UCBShift 2.0 (Dasari et al. 2024)** — ensemble of two components: UCBShift-Y (alignment to manually-assigned shift database) + UCBShift-X (random forest on handcrafted structural features). Trained on 1051 proteins (851 train, 200 test) curated from BMRB + Sparta+ + RefDB + ShiftX2. Predicts ¹H/¹³C/¹⁵N backbone + side-chain. Combines homology + structural ML.
- Klukowski explicitly notes: "deep learning is not yet dominating the field of structure-based chemical shift prediction of proteins." Potential advantages of DL: training directly on molecular graph geometry, not handcrafted features.
- **Yang et al. [19] (2021)** — message-passing GNN on molecular graph (through-bond + through-space) for ¹H/¹³C/¹⁵N. No protein- or amino-acid-specific descriptors. Training dataset RefDB + ShiftX2 + HMDB metabolomics. Performance comparable to UCBShift and SHIFTX+ with more generalizable framework. Notable: phenomenological analysis of learned representations showed the GNN inferred secondary-structure sensitivity WITHOUT being told about secondary structure — captures physicochemical relationships through learning alone.

**3.2 Chemical shifts in small molecules and molecular solids.**
- **Jonas et al. [13] (2019)** — GCNN (Kipf-Welling modified for bond types); 37-feature atom representation; 10 layers + 2 prediction heads (shift + uncertainty); NMRShiftDB2 training. MAE 0.71–1.43 ppm ¹³C, 0.09–0.28 ppm ¹H (lower for highest-certainty 10%). GCNN *outperforms DFT* on all shift types.
- **ShiftML [14] (2018)** — Gaussian Process Regression + SOAP multi-kernel. 3D neighborhood density as superposition of Gaussians at atomic positions within cutoff. 2500 CSD structures with DFT-calculated shifts. RMSEs: 0.49 ppm ¹H, 4.3 ¹³C, 13.3 ¹⁵N, 17.7 ¹⁷O (vs DFT). Scalable to 768–1584 atom systems. Versus experiment: 0.39 ppm ¹H RMSE — comparable to DFT's 0.38 ppm, but orders of magnitude faster.
- **ShiftML2 [16] (2022)** — 12-element coverage (H, C, N, O, S, F, P, Cl, Na, Ca, Mg, K) vs. 5 in ShiftML. Handles DFT-unoptimized and MD-snapshot structures. 32 kernel ridge regression ensemble with SOAP. 16,013 CSD structures. Prediction uncertainty via scaled std dev. Minor improvement on ShiftML1 test set (0.48 vs 0.46 ppm proton RMSE) but major gains on MD snapshots (0.53 vs 0.98 ppm proton RMSE along MD trajectories — *direct evidence that MD-scoring-with-ML is an active direction*).
- **3D-DenseNet [15] (2019)** — 3D grid input centered on prediction atom, captures multi-resolution environment. 4 model instances for ¹H/¹³C/¹⁵N/¹⁷O. RMSE 0.37/3.3/10.2/15.3 ppm. Outperforms kernel-regression approaches.
- **Guan et al. [12] / ExpNN-ff (2021)** — message-passing GNN for ¹H/¹³C shifts. DFTNN initial training on DFT structures + shifts, then transfer learning on experimental ¹³C, then again to eliminate DFT-optimized geometry dependency. DFTNN achieves 2.15 ppm ¹³C / 0.16 ppm ¹H RMSE. Final MAE 1.5 ppm for ¹³C. Applications: chemical-structure determination by candidate selection, automated validation of public NMR repositories, electrophilic aromatic substitution site prediction.
- **Kang et al. [11] (2020)** — weakly-supervised learning. Training data is lists of shifts observed in molecules without atom-level assignment; model predicts atom-level shifts. Increases accessible training data (experimental spectra with peak lists but no manual assignment are abundant). Mix of supervised + weakly-supervised training improves over pure supervised.

**3.3 Scalar couplings in small molecules.**
- 2019 Kaggle competition [107] on QM9 dataset (9 heavy atoms max) for ¹JHC, ¹JHN, ²JHH, ²JHC, ²JHN, ³JHH, ³JHC, ³JHN. 2700 teams, 47,800 model predictions. DFT-simulated targets. Summarized in [109]. Winner: graph transformer (GNN) with graph representation capturing atoms + bonds + atom triplets. 13-model ensemble.
- **IMPRESSION [96] (2019)** — kernel ridge regression with Coulomb matrix / aSLATM / FCHL descriptors. Predicts ¹H/¹³C shifts and ¹JCH. Trained on 882 CSD structures (active-learning-selected). MAE on test: 0.23 ppm ¹H, 2.45 ppm ¹³C, 0.87 Hz ¹JCH (vs DFT).

**3.4 Protein dynamics — directly relevant to our Stage 2.**
- Timescales (useful framing quote for us): ps–ns = vibration of chemical bonds, side-chain rotation, loop motion. μs–ms = conformational changes, inter-domain motion, folding/unfolding. Longer = aggregation and large-scale conformational changes.
- **Wang et al. [110] — order parameter S² prediction from structure ensemble alone.** Random forest (50 trees) predicts backbone ¹H-¹⁵N S² directly from NMR-structure bundle (no experimental relaxation input). Handcrafted features: Cα distance variance, torsion angle variance, SASA. Training/test: 36 proteins total (26 train, 10 test) from BMRB/PDB — order-parameter data scarcity is explicitly called out. Outperforms weighted contact-number model (WCN) [112], comparable to composite dynamic score with AlphaFold2 (cdsAF2 [113]).
- **PDBCor [114] — correlated motion from structure ensembles.** Distance matrix or torsion matrix per residue. Multiple models in NMR ensemble → GMM clustering → adjusted mutual information between residue pairs. Detects local AND global correlated motion without requiring structure superposition.

**4. Spectrum reconstruction and quality enhancement** (starts here).
- Framing: FIDs → Fourier → multidim spectra. Bottlenecks: measurement time grows 1-2 orders of magnitude per added dimension (3D NMR ~1 day), low SNR from minute Boltzmann polarization, peak splitting from scalar couplings.
- NMR theory well-understood → quantitative spectrum simulations possible → DL opportunities.

**4.1 NUS reconstructions.**
- Qu et al. [115] — dense CNN artifact suppression + iterative consistency enforcement with original NUS signal. Trained on synthetic time-domain signals subsampled via Poisson gap. 2D/3D spectra of small proteins. Comparable to low-rank reconstruction, ~10× faster. Superior fidelity at <20% NUS density.
- EDHRN [116] — Encoder-Decoder High-Resolution Network; CNN variant from HRNet [117].

**Relevance-to-our-work observations:**
- The Wang et al. [110] S² predictor from structure ensemble is directly analogous to one of our Stage-2 ticklers: predicting S² from structure without experimental relaxation data. Our proposed S²_σ fit from MD shielding trajectory is complementary — theirs is from conformational ensemble, ours is from MD dynamics. Both avoid needing BMRB relaxation.
- ShiftML2 explicitly demonstrates ML-prediction of shifts along MD trajectories — directly relevant to our Stage 2 time-series framing. Prior art for applying shift prediction to MD snapshots.
- PDBCor correlated-motion detection from ensemble without superposition is architecturally interesting — parallels our Tucker-decomposition idea (idea 4 in time-series doc) of finding structured modes in per-atom data without imposing a reference frame.
- UCBShift 2.0 detail confirms what to add to PENDING: Dasari et al. 2024 with 1051-protein training set, 92.6% automated assignment accuracy via AF2+UCBShift priors in the Klukowski 2024 companion paper.
- Yang et al. [19] GNN recovering secondary-structure sensitivity without supervision is the exact kind of finding our three-fold schtick wants — physics-interpretable learned representations. Useful precedent citation for Stage 3.
