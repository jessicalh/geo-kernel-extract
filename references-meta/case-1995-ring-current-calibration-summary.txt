Case, D.A. (1995) "Calibration of ring-current effects in proteins and nucleic acids." Journal of Biomolecular NMR 6, 341–346. Received 15 August 1995; accepted 11 September 1995. 6 pages, 34 references. Supported by NIH Grant GM45811; DeMON DFT program supplied by Salahub, Malkin & Malkina.

Case calibrates two long-standing empirical formulas for ring-current effects in biomolecular NMR — Johnson & Bovey's 1958 loop-current model and Haigh & Mallion's 1980 surface-integral Hückel model — plus a Buckingham-style electrostatic polarization term σ_el = A·E(C–H), against direct DFT shielding calculations on a model system. The output is a table of updated ring-current intensity factors and electrostatic coefficients suitable for protein and nucleic acid chemical shift prediction.

Method: a methane "probe" molecule is placed at positions in and out of the plane of 10 aromatic rings — benzene, phenol, indole, imidazole, imidazolium, adenine, guanidine, uracil, thymine, cytosine — at probe-to-ring distances 2.9 Å (van der Waals contact) to 5.5 Å. 421 secondary shifts are computed as σ(methane near ring) − σ(isolated methane) at the DFT level (DeMON program, PW91 exchange-correlation functional, IGLO-III basis), with Hartree-Fock GIAO calculations (Gaussian 94) for comparison. Ring geometries come from AMBER X-ray averages; electric fields entering σ_el are calculated from AMBER 4.1 partial charges via Coulomb's law, appropriate for gas phase.

Both empirical formulas fit the DFT shifts with Pearson r = 0.94–0.99 and rms difference < 0.1 ppm; Johnson–Bovey and Haigh–Mallion are statistically indistinguishable for scalar shifts. Updated intensity factors are generally larger than the older Giessner-Prettre & Pullman 1969/1987 values — histidine's ring current rises from 0.53 to 1.35–1.40, tryptophan's 5-membered ring from 0.56 to 1.02–1.32. The electrostatic coefficient A comes out at −3.0 ppm/au for nucleic acids and −3.4 ppm/au for protein aromatic contexts — consistent with Buckingham's 1960 value (−2×10⁻¹² e.s.u.) and with Augspurger & Dykstra's 1991 QM methane-probe estimate (−2.6×10⁻¹² e.s.u.).

Implications for our work, widely considered. This is the direct ancestor of the thesis. Our work generalizes Case 1995 along three axes: scalar isotropic shift → full rank-2 shielding tensor with T0/T1/T2 irreducible decomposition; 421 methane-probe shifts → 446K atom environments across 720 proteins for Stage 1 plus MD-averaged trajectories for Stage 2; gas-phase Coulomb with AMBER charges → explicit solvent via MD, AMBER ff14SB, and APBS. The final concern Case flags — "the best way to handle electrostatic effects in empirical shift calculations in solution has not yet been established" — is exactly the gap Stages 2 and 3 are meant to close. Most important for the thesis argument: Case's finding that Johnson–Bovey and Haigh–Mallion fit scalar shifts equally well (r = 0.99) is the starting point of the T2 novelty claim. The two models make opposing predictions for the rank-2 tensor, so calibrating them against DFT tensors — which we do, Case could not — is diagnostic in a way scalar calibration cannot be.
