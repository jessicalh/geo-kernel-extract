## Tier 1 — Foundational (cited across most of our summaries; several of our summaries lean on these)

- **Pople, J.A. (1956)** "Proton magnetic resonance of hydrocarbons." *J. Chem. Phys.* 24, 1111. → Point-dipole ring-current origin; cited by Case 1995, Sahakyan-V 2013, Agarwal 1977, Moyna 1998.
- **Johnson, C.E. & Bovey, F.A. (1958)** "Calculation of NMR spectra of aromatic hydrocarbons." *J. Chem. Phys.* 29, 1012. → Biot-Savart two-loop origin; paired with HM in our library.
- **Waugh, J.S. & Fessenden, R.W. (1957)** "NMR of hydrocarbons: the free electron model." *JACS* 79, 846. → Historical precursor to JB loop model; sideways calibration context.
- **Haigh, C.W. & Mallion, R.B. (1979/1980)** "Ring current theories in nuclear magnetic resonance." *Prog. NMR Spectrosc.* 13, 303–344. → Definitive HM surface-integral review; already identified as our HM kernel source.
- **Haigh, C.W. & Mallion, R.B. (1971)** "Proton magnetic resonance of nonplanar condensed benzenoid hydrocarbons. II. Theory of chemical shifts." *Mol. Phys.* 22, 955–970. → Earlier HM formulation, cited by Agarwal 1977 and Pelloni 2004.
- **Haigh, C.W. & Mallion, R.B. (1972)** "New tables of 'ring current' shielding in proton magnetic resonance." *Org. Magn. Reson.* 4, 203–228. → Numerical tables, historical precursor to 1980 review.
- **Lazzeretti, P. (2000)** "Ring currents." *Prog. NMR Spectrosc.* 36, 1–88. → The most complete pedagogical compilation of ring-current theory. Pelloni 2004 and Gershoni-Poranne 2015 both point here as the definitive treatment.
- **Ramsey, N.F. (1950)** "Magnetic shielding of nuclei in molecules." *Phys. Rev.* 78, 699. → Foundational paramagnetic shielding theory; Facelli's starting point. Multiple later papers reference "the 1950 Ramsey framework."
- **Marshall, T.W. & Pople, J.A. (1958)** "The hydrogen atom in a weak electric field." *Mol. Phys.* 1, 199. → H-atom shielding tensor under uniform field; the numerical anchor Buckingham 1960 uses.
- **Onsager, L. (1936)** "Electric moments of molecules in liquids." *JACS* 58, 1486. → Reaction-field model for solvent shielding effects; foundational for Buckingham 1960 and everything that builds on it.
- **Schleyer, P.v.R., Maerker, C., Dransfeld, A., Jiao, H. & van Eikema Hommes, N.J.R. (1996)** "Nucleus-Independent Chemical Shifts: A Simple and Efficient Aromaticity Probe." *JACS* 118, 6317–6318. → NICS origin paper (cited 2670+ times).
- **Buckingham, A.D. (1960)** *Mol. Phys.* 3, 219–224 — IF DIFFERENT from the Can. J. Chem. 38, 300 paper we have. → Check existence; the annotated bib entry A11 cites the Mol. Phys. version but three independent summaries confirm the Can. J. Chem. version, so this may be a single paper or a short companion. Worth verifying.

## Tier 2 — Vendruscolo group / Case lineage (directly adjacent to our thesis)

- **Ösapay, K. & Case, D.A. (1991)** "A new analysis of proton chemical shifts in proteins." *JACS* 113, 9436–9444. → The experimental shift dataset Moyna 1998 and many later calibrations use.
- **Ösapay, K. & Case, D.A. (1994)** "Analysis of proton chemical shifts in regular secondary structures of proteins." *J. Biomol. NMR* 4, 215–230. → Extends the 1991 analysis.
- **Ösapay, K., Theriault, Y., Wright, P.E. & Case, D.A. (1994)** "Solution structure of carbonmonoxy myoglobin determined from NMR distance and chemical shift constraints." *J. Mol. Biol.* 244, 183–197. → Shift-constrained protein structure, relevant to thesis framing.
- **Case, D.A., Dyson, H.J. & Wright, P.E. (1994)** *Methods in Enzymology* 239, 392–416. → Case-group methodology chapter, cited throughout.

- **Shen, Y. et al. (2008)** "Consistent blind protein structure generation from NMR chemical shift data." *PNAS* 105, 4685–4690. → CS-Rosetta precursor; Bax group.
- **Robustelli, P., Kohlhoff, K., Cavalli, A. & Vendruscolo, M. (2010)** *Structure* 18, 923–933. → Structure prediction from chemical shifts.

- **Williamson, M.P. & Asakura, T. (1992)** "Empirical comparisons of models for chemical-shift calculations in proteins." *J. Magn. Reson.* 101, 63–71. → Compares methods on a protein dataset.
- **Williamson, M.P., Kikuchi, J. & Asakura, T. (1995)** "Application of ¹H NMR chemical shifts to measure the quality of protein structures." *J. Mol. Biol.* 247, 541–546. → Shift as structure-quality proxy.
- **Kuszewski, J., Gronenborn, A.M. & Clore, G.M. (1995)** "The impact of direct refinement against proton chemical shifts on protein structure determination by NMR." *J. Magn. Reson. B* 107, 293–297. → Direct shift-based refinement, SHIFTY precursor.
- **Wishart, D.S. (2008)** "Recent applications and advances in the use of chemical shifts in structure calculations." *Nucleic Acids Res.* → RefDB/SHIFTY ecosystem reference.
- **Wishart, D.S., Sykes, B.D. & Richards, F.M. (1991)** "Relationship between NMR chemical shift and protein secondary structure." *J. Mol. Biol.* 222, 311–333. → Chemical shift indexing origin.
- **Wishart, D.S. & Case, D.A. (2001)** "Use of chemical shifts in macromolecular structure determination." *Methods in Enzymology* 338, 3–34. → Case-Wishart review chapter cited by Markwick 2010 and the Vendruscolo lineage.
- **Neal, S., Nip, A.M., Zhang, H. & Wishart, D.S. (2003)** "Rapid and accurate calculation of protein ¹H, ¹³C and ¹⁵N chemical shifts." *J. Biomol. NMR* 26, 215–240. → SHIFTX empirical predictor; load-bearing for Markwick 2010.
- **Xu, X.-P. & Case, D.A. (2001)** "Automated prediction of ¹⁵N, ¹³Cα, ¹³Cβ and ¹³C′ chemical shifts in proteins using a density functional database." *J. Biomol. NMR* 21, 321–333. → SHIFTS predictor; Case-group counterpart to SHIFTX.
- **Cervantes, C.F., Markwick, P.R.L., Sue, S.-C., McCammon, J.A., Dyson, H.J. & Komives, E.A. (2009)** "Functional dynamics of the folded ankyrin repeats of IκBα revealed by nuclear magnetic resonance." *Biochemistry* 48, 8563–8573. → Companion paper to Markwick 2010 providing the R1/R2/hetNOE IκBα dynamics data for the cross-validation.

- **Meiler, J. (2003)** "PROSHIFT: protein chemical shift prediction using artificial neural networks." *J. Biomol. NMR* 26, 25–37. → Historical neural-network predictor; cited in the lineage.
- **Li, J. et al.** "UCBShift" — Han lab, Berkeley, deep-learning predictor. Exact citation to verify on acquisition.

- **Cavalli, A., Salvatella, X., Dobson, C.M. & Vendruscolo, M. (2007)** "Protein structure determination from NMR chemical shifts." *PNAS* 104, 9615–9620. → Already in our list; re-anchored here as the CHESHIRE origin paper for the DFT-tensor-database approach.

## Tier 6 — Protein dynamics and NMR time-series methodology (Stage 2 exploratory)

These become priority after the 2026-04-24 reframe of Section 4 away from pure averaging toward per-frame time-series analysis of the (T, N, K, 9) shielding-tensor object. The "small + tooled-up" positioning licenses including methods the mainstream NMR-MD community treats as too rigorous or borrowed from elsewhere.

- **Lipari, G. & Szabo, A. (1982)** "Model-free approach to the interpretation of NMR relaxation in macromolecules." *JACS* 104, 4546–4559 and 4559–4570. → Canonical NMR time-series formalism; order parameters and autocorrelation functions. Annotated bib M1 [CORE].
- **Amadei, A., Linssen, A.B.M. & Berendsen, H.J.C. (1993)** "Essential dynamics of proteins." *Proteins* 17, 412–425. → PCA on MD trajectory; the template for decomposing time-indexed protein data into mode structure. Annotated bib M19 [CORE].
- **Grossfield, A. & Zuckerman, D.M. (2009)** "Quantifying uncertainty and sampling quality in biomolecular simulations." *Annu. Rep. Comput. Chem.* 5, 23–48. → MD convergence and block-averaging discipline. Annotated bib H7 [CORE].
- **Tugarinov, V. & Kay, L.E. (2003)** "Cross-correlated relaxation in methyl groups in high-molecular-weight proteins." *JACS* 125, 11520–11521. → Cross-correlated relaxation framing. Annotated bib M10 [CORE].
- **Kasinath, V. & Wand, A.J. (2013)** "Microscopic insights into the NMR relaxation-based protein conformational entropy meter." *JACS* 135, 15092–15100. → Order parameter as entropy meter; biological interpretation. Annotated bib M7.
- **Showalter, S.A. & Brüschweiler, R. (2007)** "Validation of molecular dynamics simulations of biomolecules using NMR spin relaxation as benchmarks." *JCTC* 3, 961–975. → MD-force-field validation via NMR dynamics data. Annotated bib H10.
- **Berne, B.J. & Harp, G.D. (1970)** "On the calculation of time correlation functions." *Adv. Chem. Phys.* 17, 63–227. → Foundational TCF treatment for MD. Annotated bib M28 [CORE].
- **Kubo, R. (1957)** "Statistical-mechanical theory of irreversible processes." *J. Phys. Soc. Japan* 12, 570. → Linear response and TCF grounding. Annotated bib M27 [CORE].
- **Zwanzig, R. (1961)** "Memory effects in irreversible thermodynamics." *Phys. Rev.* 124, 983. → Memory-kernel / Mori-Zwanzig projection. Annotated bib M29. Required if we commit to non-Markovian decomposition of the shielding series.

**Adjacent-field method references, not yet prioritised but likely needed if we commit to the corresponding direction:**
- **Kolda, T.G. & Bader, B.W. (2009)** "Tensor decompositions and applications." *SIAM Review* 51, 455–500. → Canonical review of Tucker, CP, and higher-order SVD decompositions. Open access. Required if the exploratory tensor-factorisation analysis of (T, N, K, 9) proceeds.
- **Prinz, J.-H. et al. (2011)** "Markov models of molecular kinetics: generation and validation." *J. Chem. Phys.* 134, 174105. → Canonical modern MSM reference. Required if the MSM analysis proceeds.
- **Chodera, J.D. & Noé, F. (2014)** "Markov state models of biomolecular conformational dynamics." *Curr. Opin. Struct. Biol.* 25, 135–144. → Accessible MSM review.
