## Entry 1 — Boyd and Skrynnikov 2002

The "1 ppm or better" correspondence claim is approximately right for the **isotropic** comparison (paper reports 0.99 ± 0.05 ppm for NMA-benzene vs NMA-cyclohexadiene+σ_rc). The paper separately states "rmsd variation of <1 ppm" for CSA contributions to ¹³C′ and ¹⁵N — distinct claim. Entry conflates the two but the headline number stands.

Authors' substantive claims (small backbone heavy-atom contribution, large NH→π contribution) are accurately reflected.

**Minor**: cycloheadiene → cyclohexadiene; "N--methyl" double-hyphen → single; Benzene capitalisation.

## Entry 3 — Han et al. 2011 SHIFTX2

### Issue: Conflation of two different curation pipelines
- **As written**: *"narrowing the approximately 1900 proteins in the database at time of publication to less than 250 high quality structures for predictive analysis"*
- **Paper says**:
  - *"RefDB, which is updated weekly, currently contains 1903 re-referenced protein assignments"* (chunk 1)
  - *"we compiled a preliminary collection of ~300 candidate proteins from a number of sources" → "~250 high resolution X-ray structures" → "the final training dataset consisted of 197 high resolution and high-quality protein structures"* (chunk 1)
- **Correct**: RefDB held 1,903 chemical-shift assignment records (a CHEMICAL SHIFT database). The structure-based training set was compiled from a separate ~300-protein candidate pool, narrowed to ~250 X-ray structures, finalised at 197. The 1903 → 250 → 197 narrowing as written conflates two different filtering pipelines.

## Entry 6 — Larsen et al. 2015 ProCS15

### Issue 1 (severe — inverted claim): "no grain was seen for ensemble structures"
- **As written**: *"In the tests done, no grain was seen for ensemble structures."*
- **Paper says**:
  - Abstract (chunk 1): *"These maximum RMSD values can be reduced by using NMR-derived structural ensembles of Ubiquitin. For example, for the largest ensemble the largest RMSD values are 1.7, 0.5, and 3.5 ppm for carbon, hydrogen, and nitrogen."*
  - Conclusions (chunk 5): *"The RMSD values computed using ProCS15 for Ubiquitin can be reduced by as much as 0.7, 0.1, and 0.5 ppm for carbon, hydrogen, and nitrogen by using NMR-derived structural ensembles."*
- **Correct**: Gain WAS seen for ProCS15 on ensembles — up to 0.7 / 0.1 / 0.5 ppm for C / H / N. It's the **empirical** predictors that don't gain. Entry has the polarity inverted. ("grain" also reads as a typo for "gain".)

### Issue 2: Calculation count and scope
- **As written**: *"approixmately two million tripeptide DFTs"*
- **Paper says** (abstract, chunk 1): *"around 2.35 million OPBE/6-31G(d,p)//PM6 calculations on tripeptides and small structural models of hydrogen-bonding"*
- **Correct**: ~2.35 million (not 2 million), and they include **both** tripeptide AND hydrogen-bonding model-system calculations (not just tripeptides).

### Issue 3: RMSD reference frame and protein scope
- **As written**: *"The resulting system on ubiquitin provides RMSD in the range of 2.5 ppm for C, 0.8 ppm for H, and 4.5 ppm for N."*
- **Paper says** (chunk 5): *"ProCS15 reproduces the chemical shielding values computed using PCM/OPBE/6-31G(d,p)//PM6-D3H+ for Ubiquitin and GB3 with RMSD values (after linear regression) of up to 2.5 ppm for carbon atoms, 0.8 ppm for hydrogen atoms, and 4.5 ppm for nitrogen."*
- **Correct**: 2.5 / 0.8 / 4.5 ppm are the RMSDs of ProCS15 vs **full DFT** (not vs experiment) for **both ubiquitin AND GB3**. Entry implies these are vs experiment for ubiquitin only.

**Minor**: "approixmately" → "approximately"; "no grain" → "no gain".

## Entry 8 — Sahakyan and Vendruscolo 2013

### Issue 1: Method-name spelling
- **As written**: *"Haigh-Maillon"* (appears twice — header and body)
- **Paper says**: *"Haigh−Mallion"* throughout
- **Correct**: Mallion (R. B. Mallion).

### Issue 2: "Adjacent rings" misuses the paper's taxonomy
- **As written**: *"Maximum effect of adjacent rings was calculated, with H1 at 4.84 ppm, N15 at 34.49 ppm, and C13 at 27.18 ppm."*
- **Paper says** (chunk 3): *"Overall, neglecting the dibase systems with hydrogen-bonded configurations, the overall effect of the neighboring conjugated rings on ¹H, ¹⁵N, and ¹³C nuclei in RNAs can be as high as 4.84, 34.49, and 27.18 ppm, respectively..."*
- **Correct**: The maxima are taken across **ADJ and SPT classes combined, excluding hydrogen-bonded configurations** — not "adjacent rings" alone. ADJ is a specific category in the paper's taxonomy (rings in neighboring nucleotides on the same chain), distinct from SPT (spatially close).

### Issue 3: Johnson-Bovey was reviewed, not used in the fits
- **As written**: *"point dipole, Johnson-Bovey, and Haigh-Maillon results were calculated"*
- **Paper says** (chunk 3): *"The fittings were performed in multiple schemes, applying both the Pople and Haigh−Mallion models with and without accounting the electric field effects."*
- And earlier (chunk 1): *"Although the Waugh−Fessenden−Johnson−Bovey model is differentiable, its geometric factor, unlike the ones in other models, contains an adjustable parameter that should be optimized for different conjugated systems."*
- **Correct**: Only Pople (point-dipole) and Haigh-Mallion were applied in the linear fits across the DiBaseRNA database. Johnson-Bovey is described and compared at the benzene level but not applied to the dibase chemical-shift fits. Entry's inclusion of "Johnson-Bovey" in the list of "results were calculated" is misleading.
